Super Girl updated

 From a climb, regrip the legs so that the pole is placed high in the thighs. Lean to the side to enable you to place one hand below your body on the pole in an upside down grip. Carefully shuffl e your hands down the pole to lock out the bottom arm. From here, push the hips up as you release the top hand and straighten out the body From the Super Girl position, try to lower down slowly into a hangback & handstand to dismount.

Sideways Climb

 Place the back of your strong leg tightly on the pole about hip height, and place your strong hand high on the pole with the second hand just below. Lift the second leg up on to the pole in a climb position. To climb the pole neatly, follow this process; Re-grip the strong hand, followed by the strong leg, then move second hand and second leg. Repeat process for second climb You can finish this climb with a French Kiss, or create your own move when you are there!

The Mushroom

 From the French Kiss, bend the bottom/ straight leg over the top of the other leg to increase the grip on the back of the knee. From here, lift your body up and in, towards the pole, to enable you to wrap your top elbow around the pole and take hold of the knee. You should now be secure enough to remove the bottom hand and wrap it around your legs, tuck your head in and squeeze into a ball! Play with the transition from French Kiss into the Mushroom and then back again.

Shouldermount

 Stand with your back on the pole and place the hands on in a cup grip above your head. Take the pole onto the same shoulder as your lowest hand. Ensure your head is on the opposite side of the pole. Swing and kick up with the leg that is in line with the shoulder. Once upside down you will need a quick grip with the legs to secure yourself Take this into an upside-down no hands or a Pole Cat.

Half Butterfly

 From the Caroline, slide the bottom hand down the pole so you are in a split grip, from here, turn your body so your face is in line with the pole. You should now be upside down with one leg hooked around the pole. Bend the free leg behind you to create a nice finish To perfect this pose – Create a nice arch by extending your free foot back towards your head.

Angel

 From a Star, make sure you have a secure grip in the thigh and the back of your shoulder before you slowly slide your hand, out so that both hands are off the pole Experiment with different arm positions; try taking hold of your legs with the opposite hands.

Super Man

 From an upside down, back of the knee grip, raise your torso so you are able to place your strong hand on the pole above the knee. There is no need to move or re-grip the second hand on the pole. Twist your hips as you remove the bottom hand and straighten the legs, keeping the pole between the thighs, and body horizontal Aim to make the hip twist transition smooth & extend the free arm. The body should be in a pretty horizontal pose.

Climb Into Upside Down

 From a climb, ensure both hands are on the pole at head height, take your body to the side of the pole and use the inside foot to push off from the pole to pull yourself upside down. Once you have done this a few times – try not to use the foot to push off the pole. Make sure you tilt your body backwards as you lift. The more you tuck your knees in to your chest, the easier it is Aim to do this move without using the pole to push off from. Try climbing into a helicopter and then straightening the legs.

Extended Butterfly

 From a Half Butterly position, place the bottom hand down the pole in an upside down grip, ensure both arms are straight and locked out. From here, place the arch of your foot or back of the heel onto the pole and extend the second leg away from the pole. Make sure all arms and legs are straight and extended outwards as far as you can. Pike your free leg forwards to dismount lightly Arch the back & extend the leg as far as you can but make sure you control your dismount.

Climbing Flag

 Re-visit The Flag from Advanced Plus. This time you are going to add on a climb beforehand. From the climb, bring your body around and in front of the pole to place your armpit in a tight grip and follow the exact same steps for the fl ag. Make sure you don’t jump out of this position! Come back to the pole & re grip with the legs before releasing the handgrips to slide down Experiment with leg positions as in the Advanced Plus course.

Elbow Grip

 From upside down no hands, take one arm around the pole across the body and ensure the pole is tightly gripped in the crook of the elbow. Lock the second arm out straight and place the hand on the pole with fi ngers pointing down. Slowly release the leg grip, ensuring both legs come away from the pole at the same time to keep balance. Try not to lean the legs away from the pole Practice holding this move & finding your balance. Take the legs as wide as you can to perfect it.

Underarm Grab

 Climb the pole and with hands at head height, bring your body to one side. Drag the inside arm down the pole to secure a tight grip on the underarm, and take hold of the inside of the thigh. Keep your legs wide to help distribute your weight. When secure, take the other hand off the pole and hold the position Experiment with different leg positions.

Full Butterfly

 From a Pole Cat, re-grip the hand that is nearest to your belly button to place your elbow around the pole, ensuring you keep your body away from the pole to increase the elbow grip. When you are secure, slowly release the leg that is resting on the pole, keeping your strong leg hooked on. Keep a strong pole cat position for maximum effect Link this with a move of your choice.

Box Splits

 From Half Splits, re-grip the bottom hand so it is placed just underneath your top hand. Then open the top knee grip & place the arch of your foot on the pole. You can also achieve this pose by sliding your bottom backwards as you unhook the top knee, and then slide the foot into position Try to take the legs as wide as possible, but listen to your body and know your limits.

Fireman Pull Up Spin

 As you spin and lift into this move, place one foot in front of the pole, and one behind in a climbing position. Lift your knees as high as you can & then straighten the legs to gain the height. Your strong hand needs to be high whilst the other hand is better at belly button height. Walk into this move to add the momentum. Aim to get as high as you can with the fi rst climb & experiment with the free arm to give a performance effect Try and do 2 climbs to the top. Point your toes & lift your knees high to get the height.
